# Video Platforms

A collection of the best video platforms focused on privacy, creator freedom, decentralization, open-source technology, and reduced dependence on Big Tech ecosystems.

Any video platform will work (Tiktok, etc.), but these are some that are solely focused on being open sourced and privacy focused. This still does limit the options a lot though.

---

# PeerTube

- Website: https://joinpeertube.org
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

PeerTube is a decentralized video platform powered by independent servers instead of a single company.

### Unique Benefits

- Federated video hosting
- No central authority
- Open source
- Community-owned instances
- Reduced censorship concerns

### Best For

Users who support decentralized media platforms.

---

# Invidious

- Website: https://invidious.io
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

## Why Use It?

Invidious is a privacy-focused frontend for YouTube that removes ads and tracking.

### Unique Benefits

- No Google tracking
- Lightweight interface
- No ads
- Subscription support without accounts
- Faster performance

### Best For

Users who still watch YouTube content but want more privacy.

---

# Piped

- Website: https://piped.video
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

## Why Use It?

Piped is a modern privacy-focused YouTube frontend with strong performance and clean design.

### Unique Benefits

- No ads or tracking
- Modern UI
- Background playback
- Lightweight streaming
- Subscription support

### Best For

Users wanting a cleaner YouTube experience.

---

# Odysee

- Website: https://odysee.com
- Open Source: Partially
- Privacy Focused: Moderate

## Why Use It?

Odysee is a decentralized video platform built on blockchain-based infrastructure.

### Unique Benefits

- Decentralized content hosting
- Creator-focused platform
- Reduced platform dependency
- Alternative recommendation system
- Large independent creator community

### Best For

Users wanting alternatives to mainstream creator platforms.

---

# Vimeo

- Website: https://vimeo.com
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Moderate

## Why Use It?

Vimeo focuses on high-quality video hosting for creators and professionals.

### Unique Benefits

- High-quality video compression
- Cleaner platform experience
- Professional creator tools
- Less algorithm-driven content
- Portfolio-friendly platform

### Best For

Filmmakers and creative professionals.

---

# Dailymotion

- Website: https://dailymotion.com
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Moderate

## Why Use It?

Dailymotion is one of the oldest alternatives to YouTube with global content hosting.

### Unique Benefits

- Alternative content ecosystem
- Large international audience
- Simple video hosting
- Independent platform

### Best For

Users wanting broader platform diversity.

---

# Jellyfin

- Website: https://jellyfin.org
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

## Why Use It?

Jellyfin is a self-hosted media server for streaming your own content.

### Unique Benefits

- Fully self-hosted
- No tracking
- Open source
- Stream your own media library
- No subscription required

### Best For

Users who want complete ownership of their media.

---

# Kodi

- Website: https://kodi.tv
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

Kodi is an open-source media center for organizing and streaming personal content.

### Unique Benefits

- Massive plugin ecosystem
- Local media management
- Fully customizable
- Open source
- Multi-platform support

### Best For

Home theater and media enthusiasts.

---

# YouTube

- Website: https://youtube.com
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Low

## Why Mention It?

YouTube dominates online video but comes with heavy tracking, advertising, and algorithmic influence.

### Downsides

- Extensive tracking
- Aggressive advertising
- Recommendation manipulation
- Centralized control
- Heavy data collection

### Best Alternative Replacements

- PeerTube
- Invidious
- Piped
- Odysee

# Final Thoughts

Video platforms shape entertainment, education, news, and online culture.  
Choosing better alternatives can improve:

- Privacy
- Creator independence
- Media ownership
- Freedom from recommendation algorithms
- Reduced tracking and advertising

Whenever possible, support open and decentralized media ecosystems that give users and creators more control.
