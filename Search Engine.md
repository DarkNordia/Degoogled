# Search Engines

A collection of the best search engines focused on privacy, independence, transparency, speed, and unique features.

---

# DuckDuckGo

- Website: https://duckduckgo.com
- Open Source: Partially
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

DuckDuckGo is one of the most popular privacy-focused search engines.  
It does not track your searches, build advertising profiles, or store personal search history.

### Unique Benefits

- No personalized tracking
- Cleaner search experience
- Bang shortcuts (`!yt`, `!w`, `!gh`)
- Lightweight and fast
- Simple UI

### Best For

People who want a familiar Google-like experience without aggressive tracking.

---

# Brave Search

- Website: https://search.brave.com
- Open Source: Partially
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

Brave Search has its own independent search index, meaning it is less reliant on Google or Bing results.

### Unique Benefits

- Independent search engine index
- AI answer summaries
- Strong privacy protections
- Minimal censorship concerns
- Integrated with Brave Browser

### Best For

Users who want an independent alternative to Google with modern features.

---

# SearXNG

- Website: https://searxng.org
- Open Source: Fully
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

SearXNG is a metasearch engine that combines results from multiple search providers while hiding your identity.

### Unique Benefits

- Completely open source
- Self-hostable
- No ads or tracking
- Highly customizable
- Uses multiple search providers at once

### Best For

Advanced users and privacy enthusiasts.

---

# Kagi

- Website: https://kagi.com
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

Kagi is a premium search engine focused entirely on high-quality search results instead of ads.

### Unique Benefits

- No advertisements
- Extremely high-quality results
- Custom result ranking
- Block unwanted websites
- Cleaner web experience

### Best For

People who care about search quality more than free services.

---

# Startpage

- Website: https://startpage.com
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

Startpage delivers Google search results anonymously.

### Unique Benefits

- Google-quality results
- No tracking
- Anonymous View feature
- Simple interface

### Best For

Users who still want Google results without Google tracking.

---

# Mojeek

- Website: https://www.mojeek.com
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Yes

## Why Use It?

Mojeek operates its own independent crawler and index instead of relying on larger search providers.

### Unique Benefits

- Independent search infrastructure
- No user tracking
- Unique ranking system
- More diverse search results

### Best For

People who support independent web infrastructure.

# Ecosia

- Website: https://www.ecosia.org
- Open Source: Partially
- Privacy Focused: Moderate

## Why Use It?

Ecosia uses its ad revenue to fund tree planting projects around the world.

### Unique Benefits

- Plants trees through searches
- Climate-focused company
- Transparent financial reports
- Privacy improvements over Google

### Best For

Environmentally conscious users.

---

# Qwant

- Website: https://www.qwant.com
- Open Source: Partially
- Privacy Focused: Yes

Qwant is a European privacy-focused search engine that avoids personalized tracking.

### Unique Benefits

# Final Thoughts

No search engine is perfect for everyone.  
The best option depends on whether you prioritize:

- Privacy
- Open source software
- Search quality
- Decentralization
- Environmental impact
- Independence from Big Tech

Experiment with multiple search engines and decide which fits your workflow best.
