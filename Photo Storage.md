# Photo Storage

A collection of the best photo storage platforms focused on privacy, ownership, backup reliability, open-source software, and freedom from centralized cloud ecosystems.

---

## Immich

- Website: https://immich.app
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

### Why Use It?

Immich is a self-hosted photo and video backup platform inspired by Google Photos.

### Unique Benefits

- Self-hosted photo backups
- Automatic mobile uploads
- Facial recognition support
- Beautiful modern UI
- Full ownership of media

### Best For

Users wanting a Google Photos replacement.

---

## Ente

- Website: https://ente.io
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

### Why Use It?

Ente provides encrypted cloud photo storage with a polished user experience.

### Unique Benefits

- End-to-end encryption
- Cross-platform syncing
- Automatic backups
- Privacy-focused design
- Open-source apps

### Best For

Users wanting secure cloud photo backups.

---

## PhotoPrism

## Nextcloud Photos

### Unique Benefits

- Self-hosted storage

## Syncthing

- Website: https://syncthing.net
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

### Why Use It?

Syncthing directly syncs photos between devices without cloud providers.

### Unique Benefits

- Peer-to-peer syncing
- No central servers
- Fast local transfers
- Complete ownership
- Open source

### Best For

Users avoiding cloud storage entirely.

## Google Photos

- Website: https://photos.google.com
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Low

### Why Mention It?

- Cloud dependence

## Final Thoughts

Photo storage platforms often contain years of personal memories and sensitive media.  
Choosing better alternatives can improve:

- Privacy
- Backup reliability
- Data ownership
- Long-term storage control
- Freedom from centralized cloud ecosystems

Whenever possible, prioritize platforms that offer encryption, transparency, and full control over your media.
