# Productivity Apps

A collection of the best productivity apps focused on organization, collaboration, privacy, open-source software, and freedom from Big Tech ecosystems.

---

## Notion

- Website: https://notion.so
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: Moderate

### Why Use It?

Notion combines notes, databases, task management, and collaboration into one flexible workspace.

### Unique Benefits

- Extremely flexible workspace system
- Databases and organization tools
- Team collaboration
- Templates and customization
- Clean modern UI

### Best For

Students, teams, and organization-heavy workflows.

---

## Obsidian

- Website: https://obsidian.md
- Open Source: No
- Privacy Focused: High

### Why Use It?

Obsidian stores notes locally using Markdown files with deep linking and knowledge management tools.

### Unique Benefits

- Local-first storage
- Markdown note system
- Graph visualization
- Massive plugin ecosystem
- Offline support

### Best For

Knowledge management and long-term note taking.

## Final Thoughts

Productivity apps often store your notes, projects, ideas, and personal workflows.  
Choosing better alternatives can improve:

- Privacy
- Data ownership
- Offline functionality
- Customization
- Freedom from centralized ecosystems

Local-first and open-source tools are often the best long-term choices for maintaining control over your information.
