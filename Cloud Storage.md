# Cloud Storage

A collection of the best cloud storage platforms focused on privacy, security, ownership, synchronization, and open-source software.

---

# Nextcloud

- Website: https://nextcloud.com
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

## Why Use It?

Nextcloud is a self-hostable cloud platform that gives you full control over your files, data, and collaboration tools.

### Unique Benefits

- Fully self-hostable
- Complete ownership of data
- File syncing and sharing
- Integrated office tools
- Massive plugin ecosystem

### Best For

Users who want maximum control and privacy.

---

- Website: https://proton.me/drive

- Privacy Focused: Extremely

## Why Use It?

Proton Drive focuses on encrypted cloud storage with a simple and secure experience.

### Unique Benefits

- End-to-end encryption
- Privacy-focused ecosystem
- Secure file sharing
- Based in Switzerland
- No tracking or ads

### Best For

Users who prioritize privacy and encryption.

# Syncthing

- Website: https://syncthing.net
- Open Source: Yes
- Privacy Focused: Extremely

## Why Use It?

Syncthing directly syncs files between your devices without needing a central cloud server.

### Unique Benefits

- Peer-to-peer syncing
- No central servers
- Fully open source
- Fast local transfers
- Complete data ownership

### Best For

Users who want cloud syncing without using cloud providers.

# Final Thoughts

Cloud storage controls your files, backups, photos, and documents.  
Choosing the right platform can improve:

- Privacy
- Security
- File ownership
- Cross-device syncing
- Independence from Big Tech

If privacy matters to you, prioritize services with encryption, transparency, and open-source infrastructure whenever possible.
